The City Divided

Minneapolis as a Case Study in De Facto Segregation

The City Divided

This piece was born as a response to an opinion piece in the Minnesota Star Tribune that claimed race wasn’t a factor in city planning and considering it in city processes only alienated white residents. I decided to learn about Minneapolis’s city planning processes, racial distribution, and its 2040 Plan to understand if there was really a case where race didn’t come into play. This story map moves through Minneapolis’s history of redlining, current zoning ordinances, and the proposed built land uses of the 2040 Plan to show the spatial patterns and processes that have created and reproduced de facto segregation in Minneapolis.
